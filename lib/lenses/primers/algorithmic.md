# Algorithmic lens

## Definition

The algorithmic lens reads the meme as shaped by the recommender systems that selected for it. It asks: what features made this meme algorithmically viable, what kind of meme could no longer exist because the algorithm no longer rewards it, what pressures has the recommender exerted on the meme's *form* (length, aspect ratio, opening seconds, captioning style, hook density), and what is being filtered out we cannot see. Memes do not just spread — they are *selected*. The algorithm is the environment that exerts evolutionary pressure on meme form, and any meme analysis that ignores the algorithm reads an organism without its ecology.

## Essential moves

- Identify the **platform-native form** the meme has adapted to: TikTok's first-three-seconds hook, Instagram's vertical-square trade-off, Twitter's screenshot-aspect, Reddit's upvote-curve.
- Read the meme's **algorithmic affordances**: is it short, captioned, looping, captionable, remixable, soundtrackable, comment-baiting? These are not stylistic choices; they are selection pressures.
- Apply **selection logic**: ask what *did not* get made because the algorithm would not have surfaced it. The absences are the strongest evidence the lens has.
- Name the **moderation regime** (Gillespie) — what is the platform's content policy doing to this meme, visibly or invisibly?
- Use **Noble** when relevant: what does this algorithm surface, what does it suppress, and along which axes of race / gender / class / language?
- Apply **Chun's habituation** — the algorithm trains the user to expect more of the same, and the meme is the form that habituation produces.
- Pasquale's **opacity**: we do not know exactly how this surfaced, and the not-knowing is a governance condition, not a research limitation.
- Resist the temptation to attribute *intention* to the algorithm. The algorithm has selection pressure, not agency.

## Key theorists and short illustrative quotes

- **Tarleton Gillespie**, *Custodians of the Internet* (2018): platforms "make decisions about what is included and what is excluded, what is acceptable and what is unacceptable, what should be promoted and what should be hidden." Each meme is the residue of those decisions.
- **Safiya Umoja Noble**, *Algorithms of Oppression* (2018): on the systematic biases of search-and-surface — the meme that gets seen is the meme the algorithm has decided is seeable.
- **Wendy Hui Kyong Chun**, *Updating to Remain the Same* (2016): on habit as the underlying logic of new media. The algorithm produces habit, and the meme is habit's outward face.
- **Frank Pasquale**, *The Black Box Society* (2015): opacity as a form of governance, not a feature of analysis.
- **Kate Crawford**, *Atlas of AI* (2021): the material substrate of algorithmic systems — useful for tying the algorithmic lens to the infrastructural one.

## Worked example openings (cathedral voice)

- "This meme exists because TikTok's first-second retention curve rewards a hook this specific; a longer-burning version of the same joke was made and did not survive."
- "Read the meme as the algorithm's child, and the cathedral framing inverts: the lord did not produce the icon, but the lord's selection pressure did."
- "What the algorithm filtered out is invisible by definition, but the missing memes are the strongest evidence the lens has — name the absences before reading the presences."
